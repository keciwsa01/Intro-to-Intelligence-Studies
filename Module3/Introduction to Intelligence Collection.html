Introduction to Intelligence Collection: The Foundation of Understanding

What is Intelligence Collection?

The foundation of intelligence work, without which officers would be "basically guessing"
  • Encompasses Intelligence, Surveillance, and Reconnaissance (ISR) in the military context
Intelligence: The broad act of gathering information
Surveillance: Watching something over time
Reconnaissance: A one-time mission to gather information on a target

Historical Roots of Intelligence Gathering

  • Always paramount to fighting and winning wars
  • Civil War era showcased the vitality of accurate and timely intelligence
  • Telegraphy revolutionized military communication
    Allowed near real-time, two-way interaction and command control
  • Necessity for cryptologic methods
    Stager's Telegraphic Cipher Book
    Cipher disks for secure communication
    Due to easy interception of telegraph and visual signals
  • Early aerial telegraphy (wig-wag)
    For battlefield communication, also requiring encryption
  • The need for secure relaying of information pushed cryptologic development and skilled communication officers
